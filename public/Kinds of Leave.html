Kinds of Leave

  1. Leave Type 1 - Sick Leave:

a. Employees who are not covered in the ESI act  are entitled to sick leave to address personal illness or medical appointments provided that the said employee has completed 12 months of service in the company.

b. The company grants 10 number of sick leave days per year sick leave days per calendar year to employees.

c. Employees shall be eligible for a maximum of 3 days Sick leave per month and if unapplied, these can be accrued to the following month.

d. On events where the leave exceeds 3 days, the employee in question will be required to produce the medical certificate upon resuming work.

e. Non-Utilized Sick leaves will not be carried forward or encashed. Pending leaves will get lapsed at the end of every calendar year.

f. Sick leaves cannot be clubbed with Casual leave but can be availed along with earned leaves with due authorization of Granting authority.

 

  1. Leave Type 2 - Casual Leave:

a. Casual leave admissible to an employee is 12 days for a calendar year, subject to the condition that not more than 3 days’ casual leave may be allowed at a time.

b. Casual leave will be credited to the employees at the beginning of the calendar year(January) and can be availed on Pro-rata basis.

c. Casual leave cannot be combined with Sick leave, Earned leave, Maternity leave and Paternity leaves.

d. Sundays and Holidays falling during a period of Casual Leave are not counted as part of Casual Leave.

e. Sundays//restricted holidays/weekly offs can be prefixed/ suffixed to Casual Leave/Earned Leave/Sick Leave.

f. Casual Leave can be taken while on official trip, but no daily allowance will be admissible for the period.

g. Casual leave can be taken for half-day also.

h. If any leave is availed in the forenoon, then the person must join duty by the end of lunch hours (01:00 pm in case of general duty).

i. If any leave is availed in the afternoon, then the person must leave only when lunch hours commence (01:00 pm in case of general duty)

j. Casual leave cannot be accumulated. Leave not availed in a particular calendar year will lapse at the end of that year.

k. If a person joins in the middle of a year, casual leave will be granted to him proportionately. As far as possible Casual Leave should not be kept unutilised till December. Availing the accumulated casual leave in the last month results in disruption of work.

 

  1.  Leave Type 3 - Earned Leave:

a. Employees who have completed a minimum of 240 days service in a calendar year are entitled to Earned Leave.

b. These minimum 240 days do not include Maternity/Paternity leaves, Accidents in the workplace and Lockout.

c. Calculation of Earned Leave will be one day for every 20 working days(fractions will be ignored).

d. A maximum of 14 days per year will be allowed as EL provided that the employee is physically present for the required number of days as cited by the management.

e. Only 30 days will be allowed to carry forward to the next year. Rest will be encashed.

f. Employees should get permission from their department head/supervisor 7 days prior to the date of leave applied for.

g. Earned leave can be availed only for 3 occasions per year.

h. Days of unauthorised absence and days on which the employee is on loss of pay shall not be considered for the purpose of calculating leave eligibility.

 

  1. Leave Type 4 - Compensatory Off

a. When employees work beyond their regular working hours, on weekends, or during public holidays, they might be eligible for "compensatory off" hours.

b. Compensatory off can be availed only when the supervisor at his/her discretion requests the employee to work on holiday/weekend. The employee cannot decide to work on a holiday and then seek compensatory off.

c. Supervisor/HOD should intimidate an employee and mark a copy to the HR department. if any employee needs to work on public holiday or weekly off in advance offering his approval for the same with a short justification.

d. HR shall verify employee’s presence on said days by the matrix system/attendance/security register.

e. The supervisor will also be held accountable in case records show that an employee who was expected to turn up on a holiday did not turn up for work.

f. Supervisors are advised to use their discretion and minimise instances of Compensatory off requirements. This is to enable employees to have time to rest every week from work.

g. If an employee is working during weekly off or public holiday, he/she needs to work :

i. Chennai, TN:

1. Minimum of 4 hours for 1/2 day’s compensatory off.

2. Minimum of 8 hours for 1 day’s compensatory off.

ii. AP:

1. Minimum of 3 hours for 1/2 day’s compensatory off.

2. Minimum of 6 hours for 1 day’s compensatory off.

j. Compensatory off that involve Travel/Tour:

i. If any employee travels for official purposes during his/her week off/public holiday it should involve meeting clients, spending time at the site.

ii. Time spent travelling will not be considered as part of working on a holiday/week off.

 

 

 

  1. Leave Type 5 - Menstrual Leave:

a. This is applicable only for female AP employees who work in nurseries and farms.

b. All menstruating employees, regardless of their designation or employment type (full-time, part-time, contractual), are eligible for one menstrual leave.

c. Menstrual leave is not deducted from sick leave or annual leave balances.

d. Employees should inform their immediate supervisor or the HR department of their intention to take menstrual leave as early as possible to allow for adequate work adjustments.

e. Gourmet Popcornica encourages a supportive environment where all employees, regardless of gender, understand and respect the purpose of menstrual leave.

f. Employees facing severe symptoms that require more than the allocated leave duration may utilise their sick leave or discuss alternative arrangements with the HR department.

 

  1. Leave Type 6 - Maternity Leave

a. Admissible to female employees during:

i. Pregnancy: 180 days. - Admissible only to employees with less than two surviving children.

ii. Miscarriage/abortion (induced or otherwise): Total of 45 days in the entire service. Admissible irrespective of the number of surviving children.

b. The application in ESS should be supported by a certificate from a Registered Medical Practitioner.

c. It is counted as service for increments.

d. It is counted as service for pension. It is not admissible for ‘threatened abortion’.

e. The employee has to submit the hospital discharge certificate and a copy of birth certificate of the child after availing the maternity leave.

 

  1. Leave Type 7 - Paternity Leave

a. There is no provision on paternity leave in Indian labour law for private sector workers, but as per our policy, this kind of leave is adjusting a maximum of 50%  against the sick leave balance of the employee.

b. The proper approval of the concerned head/ Reporting Manager along with doctor's certificates should be forwarded to the HR department.
