2. Exceeding three late arrivals over 15 minutes triggers mandatory half-day unpaid leave(as decided by the Management); continued tardiness may lead to further disciplinary actions.

3. The Head of the Department has to ensure that:

a. Employees take a lunch break during specified hours (i.e. between 1:00 pm to 1:45 pm).

b. Employees take not more than 2 tea breaks during the stipulated time slots which are: 10:30am to 10:45am and/or 3:30pm to 3:45pm.

c. If any employee has a practice in taking breaks beyond the stipulated timings or late coming to work, he/she will be warned duly and action will be taken against him / her by the HR / Personnel Department.

d. The Employees follow office time while coming to the office and leaving the office.

e. The Employees punch their presence via the biometric system in the premises.

4. If an employee does not improve on his punctuality; the Management may initiate disciplinary actions against the concerned employee in addition to the debiting of half-a-day’s Casual leave to his account.

5. It will be the responsibility of the HoD to inform the HR office regarding subordinates who remain absent from duty without informing or prior permission of the concerned authority. Such unauthorised absence, if not condoned, can mean break-in-service.

6. The HoD should inform the HR Office, if an employee has obtained permission to attend late or to leave the office early.
