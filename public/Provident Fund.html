Provident Fund

The Employee Provident Fund (EPF) scheme is designed to provide social security benefits to employees. As per the Employees Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act of 1952, all employees will be covered under this scheme. The current contribution rate is set at 12% of the earned gross salary except HRA and OT. It is also restricted only up to Rs. 1800/- This amount will be deducted from the employee's salary, and an equal contribution will be made by the company, which will then be deposited into each employee's EPF account.

  1. PF Process

a. Upon joining the organisation, employees are required to complete the PF form included in the joining form. This form captures relevant details related to the Provident Fund.

b. The HR department will carefully verify and process the filled PF forms to ensure accuracy and compliance.

c. Once the PF account number is obtained, it will be duly recorded and documented for future reference.

d. All relevant details pertaining to the PF, including the employee's contributions and related information, will be diligently documented and maintained for records and future use.

  1. PF Monthly Contribution

a. PF contribution details are directly extracted from the payroll system.

b. PF contributions are processed automatically and accurately during each payroll cycle.

c. The finance department receives real-time updates on PF contribution statements for verification.

d. Digital tools analyse the statements, flagging any inconsistencies for immediate attention.

e. PF contributions are remitted to the designated PF authority within the specified time frame, as per regulatory requirements.

f. Real-time reporting and automated notifications ensure transparency and compliance.

g. Employees have access to a user-friendly self-service portal, allowing them to view their PF contributions, account balances, and download relevant statements.
