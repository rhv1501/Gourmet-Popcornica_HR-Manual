Redressal Process

1. Receipt of Complaint:

a. Any employee who experiences or witnesses sexual harassment should promptly report the incident to their reporting manager, Human Resources (HR) department.

b. Complaints can be made orally or in writing, and the complainant should provide relevant details, including date, time, location, and a description of the incident(s) to the designated person.

2. Confidentiality and Support:

a. The designated person will ensure strict confidentiality throughout the process, protecting the privacy of the complainant, witnesses, and the accused to the extent possible.

b. The complainant will be provided with information about support services available, such as counselling, employee assistance programs, or legal advice, if required.

3. Investigation

a. The Investigation Committee will conduct a fair and unbiased investigation, ensuring the rights of all parties involved.

b. The complainant, witnesses, and the accused will be interviewed separately, and relevant evidence will be collected.

c. The investigation process will be completed within a reasonable timeframe while ensuring the thoroughness of the investigation.

d. The Investigation Committee will analyse the evidence gathered and determine if sexual harassment has occurred based on the preponderance of evidence standard.

e. If sexual harassment is established, the Investigation Committee will recommend appropriate disciplinary actions in line with the company's policies and applicable laws.

f. The recommendations may include warnings, suspension, termination, or any other appropriate measures to address the situation and prevent future incidents.

4. Communication and Follow-Up

a. The HR department or designated person will communicate the outcome of the investigation and any actions taken to the complainant, the accused, and relevant stakeholders in a confidential manner.

b. The HR department will provide necessary support and assistance to the complainant, ensuring their well-being and facilitating their reintegration into the workplace.

5. Regular Review and Training

a. The company will periodically review the effectiveness of the sexual harassment policy and the redressal process to ensure continuous improvement.

b. Regular training programs will be conducted to educate employees about their rights, responsibilities, and the prevention of sexual harassment in the workplace.
