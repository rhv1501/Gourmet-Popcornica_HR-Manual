Type of employment

Definition of an employee:

Those who are directly employed for wages by the Principal Employer within the premises or outside in connection with the manufacturing process of the factory or establishment.

Definition of the Employee / Workman

  • Regular
  • Permanent
  • Probationers
  • Contractual
  • Temporary
  • Board of Apprentices (Diploma Apprentices are can engage in this category)
  • Short term Trainee.
  • Fixed term employees / Flexi contract employees.

Different categories of Company Employee here as under

Regular employee: All employees, who are working for Company and engaged directly on Company Roll, whether on training period, probationer or permanent employee.

Permanent employee: An employee, who is currently working for Company and is engaged directly on Company roll and has completed his/her training/probation period and also has been provided Employment Agreement in writing/Confirmation Letter.

Probationer: An employee who is provisionally employed on trial to ascertain his/her suitability to fill a vacancy in a permanent post and has not been confirmed as Permanent in writing by the management. All regular appointments against permanent vacancy shall ordinarily be on probation for a period of 6 (six) months. He / She will be confirmed only after completion of 6 (six) months of probation period. During this period, the employment may be separated after 15 days of notice. The employee may also resign from the services of the Company by giving 15 days notice in writing. At the end of the probationary period, we will conduct an evaluation to assess the employee's suitability within the company. The confirmation of employment with the company will be communicated in writing on the final day of the probationary period.

Contractual employee: An employee, who is engaged for - casual nature of work or a specific job for the Company through another agency, will only be for a limited period like Below 240 days, as per Act.

Temporary Employee / Short term employees is an employee who is appointed for a limited period mentioned in the letter of appointment, as per Act. (Temporary period is of less than 7 months) Labour Act.

(A) An employee who is appointed for work of a temporary nature shall not intend to mean that the employee will continue to be employed in the Company on a permanent basis.

A temporary employee will have no right or lien for regular employment. The services of a temporary employee will automatically come to an end on the expiry of the period or on completion of the work / assignment for which he/ she was appointed, whichever is earlier

  1. Employment Policies

Confidentiality

The protection of confidential business information and trade secrets is vital to the interests and success of VIPL. All employees from the cadre of Supervisors/Team Leaders are required to sign a Non-Disclosure Agreement as a condition of employment. Employees who improperly use or disclose trade secrets or confidential business information will be subject to disciplinary action, including termination of employment and legal action, even if they do not actually benefit from the disclosed information.

Such confidential information includes, but is not limited to, the following examples:

  • Compensation data
  • Financial information
  • Marketing strategies
  • Pending projects and proposals
  • Personnel/Payroll records and
  • Conversations between any persons associated with the company

Anyone found to be engaging in unlawful discrimination will be subject to disciplinary action, including termination of employment, other legal action, etc.

Employees should not publish any article or statement, deliver any lecture or broadcast or make any communication to the press, including magazine publication relating to the Company's service / business or to any matter with which the Company may be concerned, unless they have previously applied to and obtained the written permission from the Company. Employees will be required to maintain utmost secrecy in respect of Project documents, commercial offer, all documents, financial status, cost & Estimation, license, Company’s policies, Company’s Trade Mark and Company’s Human assets profile, etc. Employees will be required to comply with all such rules and regulations as the Company may frame from time to time.

Workdays and Hours of Work

The period and hours of work, for which an employee shall be required to work, will be, as per

the Law applicable to Company in this regard from time to time.

Corporate

The company will have a standard working week, starting on Monday and ending on Saturday. Corporate employees will have a 60-minute lunch break between 12:30 PM and 2:00 PM (Any 60-minute). The official working hours are from 09.30 am to 6:00 pm. The 2nd and 4th Saturdays of each month will be designated as days off

Plant/Factory

Employees will have a 6-day, 54-hour working week, Monday through Saturday. Official working hours vary by department:

General: 09.30 am to 6:00 pm.

Executive & Engineers - Production, Maintenance, Stores, Cut n Bend: 9:00 am to 6:00 pm.

Security, HR, Dispatch, Yard, Finance & Accounts, Weigh Bridge & Billing: 8:30 am to 5:30 pm.

Employees working in a 12-hour shift will continue at their assigned timing. A 60-minute lunch break is mandatory, which can be taken anytime between 12:30 PM and 2:00 PM. Employees must punch in/out during this break.

Flexibility in work hours is possible as per business requirements and assignments. Employees working late (more than 2 hours or overnight due to emergencies) can arrive 2 hours late the next day, subject to approval by GM (Factory) and notification to HR.

Attendance Policy

Biometric System Usage:

Mandatory Requirement: Every employee is required to punch in and out daily on the BioMetric device.

Penalty for Non-Compliance: Failure to punch in or out will result in Leave Adjustment or Loss of Pay (LOP).

Regularization of missed punches is allowed up to 3 times in a month.

Do's and Don'ts for Biometric System:

  • Do Register Accurately: Ensure your biometric data (e.g., fingerprints, facial recognition) is accurately registered during the initial setup. Inaccuracies can lead to attendance discrepancies.
  • Do Report Issues: If you encounter any problems with the biometric system, such as technical glitches or inaccuracies, report them immediately to the HR or IT department.
  • Don't Tamper with the Scanner: Refrain from tampering with or attempting to modify the biometric scanner or associated equipment.
  • Don't Forget to Clock In/Out: Always remember to use the system to clock in and out accurately. Forgetting to do so can result in attendance discrepancies and affect your pay.


Grace Period and Late Arrivals:

  • A grace period of 15 minutes after the reporting time is allowed, with a maximum of 4 times in a month.
  • Any arrival time beyond this grace period is considered late.
  • Three late arrivals within a month equate to half a day's leave or Loss of Pay.

On-Duty Assignments:

  • On-Duty assignments require prior intimation to the Department Head and HR.
  • For direct client calls, inform the HR department in advance to avoid being marked as late.
  • On-duty requests should be applied through the ESS portal in advance.

Leave Management

The ESS Portal will be used for managing leaves. Employees must apply for CL, SL, or EL through the ESS Portal, with prior notification to their Reporting Manager and HR. Leave taken will be adjusted/deducted from the employee's leave balance. Absence without notification is considered Loss of Pay (LOP).

Permission

Employees are entitled to 6 hours of permission per month, which can be taken in increments of up to 2 hours per day, with a maximum of three instances per month. Permission requests must be made to and approved by the employee's reporting manager, ideally in advance. The total permission time utilized should not exceed the allotted 6 hours within the month. Misuse or exceeding this allowance without proper approval may result in disciplinary actions, including deductions from leave balances or salary.

Emergency and Exceptional Circumstances

In events like severe weather, fires, or power failures, the decision to close the office will be made by the Human Resource Department. Employees will receive official notification from their functional heads in such cases. Absence without sanctioned leave for 7 days continuously, or absconding for 3 days, may lead to termination, following the issuance of a Show Cause Notice.


Work from Home

We recognize the importance of providing flexibility to our employees while maintaining productivity and meeting business objectives. This Work from Home (WFH) policy outlines the guidelines and procedures for employees who may be eligible to work remotely. The goal is to establish a structured framework that promotes a productive and balanced work environment.

Eligibility

WFH arrangements are not applicable to all positions and employees. Eligibility for WFH will be determined based on the following criteria:

  • Job Role: Some positions may require physical presence on-site and are not eligible for WFH. Eligibility is determined by the nature of the job.
  • Performance: Employees must have a history of consistent, satisfactory performance in their current role.
  • Technology and Equipment: Employees must have access to the necessary technology, equipment, and a reliable internet connection to perform their job duties remotely.
  • Managerial Approval: WFH arrangements must be approved by the employee's immediate supervisor and the HR department.

Request and Approval Process

To initiate a WFH arrangement, employees must follow these steps:

  • Submission of Request: Employees should submit a formal request to their immediate reporting manager, outlining the reason for the request, the proposed duration, and the proposed WFH schedule.
  • Reporting Manager: Reporting Manager will evaluate the request, considering the eligibility criteria and operational needs of the team and company.
  • HR Approval: Approved requests will be forwarded to the HR department for final approval and documentation.

Work Schedule

Employees working remotely are expected to adhere to the following:

  • Work Hours: Maintain regular working hours as specified.
  • Availability: Be reachable during working hours and participate in virtual meetings, as required.
  • Communication: Maintain consistent communication with team members, reporting manager, and colleagues using company-approved communication tools.

Equipment and Security

  • Employees are responsible for ensuring the security and functionality of their remote workspace, including:
  • Equipment: Ensure access to necessary equipment and software required to perform job duties.
  • Data Security: Follow company policies on data security, confidentiality, and the safe use of equipment.


Performance Evaluation

Managers will continue to assess and monitor the performance and productivity of employees working remotely. Regular performance reviews and check-ins will be conducted to ensure that work standards and expectations are met.

  1. Code of conduct and Ethics

Basic Objectives

  • To inform and remind everyone of the accepted standards of conduct and behavior at the workplace and their relationship with others;
  • To implement a clear and consistent policy on offenses and penalties in order to avoid inconsistencies arising from subjective interpretations.

Serious Misconduct:

Serious misconduct refers to certain things or actions which are by nature so serious that it places the employee outside the realm of the corrective action program.  When an employee commits any such act(s), he / she may be subjected to appropriate disciplinary measures including termination of employment.

Infraction against Persons

  • Any boxing/ physical fighting or deliberate attempt to inflict or cause bodily harm or injury upon another; or, have in fact inflicted or caused bodily harm to another either personally or through other persons, within the company premises, and outside the premises and for any reason.
  • Threats, intimidation, or coercion against any employee or persons of authority.
  • Use of abusive language or spreading false rumors or malicious statements against another employee or person of authority or against the Company.
  • Sexual Harassment
  • Committing or engaging in any indecent act, immoral conduct, illicit relationship or any act of lasciviousness, including but not limited to, any unwanted or unwelcome sexual advances, demands or requests for sexual favors, verbal or physical conduct of a sexual or otherwise offensive nature, the display of sexually suggestive objects or pictures, offensive comments, jokes, innuendoes, or other sexually oriented statement, made by an employee to another employee, applicant, trainee or any person dealing with the company
  • Stealing from another employee or any other third party

Infraction against Company Interest and Policy

  • Any act constituting falsification, misrepresentation, tampering, fraudulently altering personnel/employment records, files, time records and other personnel and/ or any company documents.
  • Any act of stealing or theft of company property, including the malicious transfer of company property without proper authorization and documentation.
  • Leaking and/ or disclosing confidential or classified Company information, personal gratification from vendors, sub-contractors or others with whom the Company has business/ contractual interest.
  • Deliberate concealment of conflict of  interest situations in order to have personal gain.
  • Mis-use of company funds, including the tampering of any documents.
  • Leave without pay in excess of five (5) days within a twelve (12) month period which has not been authorized/ratified.
  • Chronic late coming in the opinion of the Management
  • Wasting time, loitering, or abandoning work area/station during work hours without proper permission from immediate superior.
  • Sleeping on the job while on duty or within company time.
  • Deliberately slowing down or restricting operation/production and/or engaging in sabotage.
  • Insubordination or willful disobedience of superior's orders, requests including refusal to accept job assignments.
  • Inexcusable negligence, carelessness, or lack of knowledge resulting in quality or safety problems, accidents or work disruptions.
  • Failure to render overtime work or report for holiday work after being scheduled and agreeing to report for work.
  • Reporting for work without proper uniform, safety PPE (Shoes, Belts, Helmet, Gloves, safety devices/ tools and or gadgets wherever specified/applicable.
  • Reporting for work under the influence of liquor or prohibited drugs.
  • Using or operating company equipment, machinery, or vehicle without proper authorization and documentation:
  • not resulting in damages or injury to self or others
  • resulting in damage and/or personal injury to self or others
  • Creating or contributing to unsanitary conditions and/or poor housekeeping practices within the plant, office, and housing facilities.
  • Neat & Clean

Infractions Against Security, Public Order, and Public Morals

  • Deliberate disregard and/or violation of company security and safety rules and regulations.
  • Use, peddling, and/or distribution of prohibited drugs.
  • Being found guilty of a serious crime.
  • Possession and/or distribution of printed materials which are illegal, subversive, immoral and/or unauthorized by Management within company premises.
  • Providing false statements.
  • Forcing entry into the plant, office, and/or any of Company locations without proper authorization.
  • Failure to wear company ID and/or refusal to submit to security checks instituted by the Company.
  • Possession and/or concealment of firearms and/or deadly weapons within company premises.
  • Deliberately defacing, damaging and/ or destroying company property.
  • Showing/exhibiting pornographic materials, pictures, and/or literature within company premises.
  • Having a serious contagious disease which can endanger other employees and deliberately concealing it from Management and medical staff.
  • Drinking or drunkenness or intoxication, drug abuse within company premises.

Types of Penalties

The following shall be the penalties which will be imposed depending on the nature of the misconduct and the frequency of the infraction:

  1. Warning:
  • Informal one-on-one meeting between the employee and immediate superior.
  • Discussion on problem areas and ways of improvement or corrective measures.
  • May involve counseling in the presence of the respective Departmental Manager.

  1. Severe Warning:
  • Written notice with a stern warning.
  • Given after an employee's failure to correct an earlier performance problem or for serious misconduct.
  • Indicates that repetition will be dealt with more severely.
  1. Memo:
  • Formal acknowledgment and apology for recent inappropriate and unprofessional behavior at work.
  1. Fine:
  • Monetary penalty imposed on the employee.
  1. Increment Cut:
  • Reduction in salary increment.
  1. Demotion:
  • Employees are moved to a lower position with reduced responsibilities.
  1. Dismissal or Termination:
  • Cessation of the employee-employer relationship initiated by the Company.
  • Represents the most severe penalty.

Infractions and Penalties

Penalties shall be imposed only upon its confirmation by the Department Heads for Corporate Office employees and the General/Plant Manager for employees at Plant and approved by Joint CEO and CEO. The Department Heads/ General/Plant Manager shall determine the level of evidence required for substantiating the claim and also maintain records pertaining to the offense. The penalty shall become part of the employee's personal file and shall be considered a factor for performance evaluation within a twelve (12) month period.

Non-compliance results in escalating consequences, including verbal and written warnings, suspension, and possible penalties. In addition, employees are responsible for the cost of replacement if uniforms are lost or significantly torn due to negligence. Failure to wear the required uniform may also result in disciplinary actions as outlined. Employees have the right to appeal disciplinary actions.

Other Infractions

The Company may, from time to time, formulate new/other policies and guidelines which will form part and parcel of this Code of Conduct and Behavior after considering employees’ inputs and suggestions. Unauthorized absence

An employee shall not absent himself from his duties without having first obtained the permission of the reporting officer unless otherwise he/she is compelled to do so under exceptional circumstances

Conflict of Interest

  • This refers to a situation when an COMPANY employee
  • Has a financial interest in a transaction involving the Company.
  • Gains personal enrichment through access to confidential information
  • Uses his position in order to secure personal gain
  • Other situations analogous to the above
  • Any transaction with a Related party which shall mean and  include (a) an organization of which he is  an officer or director; (b) an organization of which he is the beneficial owner of ten percent (10%) or more in any manner (c) any trust in which he has a substantial interest, or serves as trustee or in a similar fiduciary capacity; or (d) any relative of him may significantly influence or be influenced by a business transaction with an organization of which he or she is an officer or director.
  • Conflict of interest also arises when an employee has a personal interest (direct or indirect) in any supplier, customer, or competitor of COMPANY.
  • A conflict of interest policy may indeed include a provision stating that employees should not belong to any political parties or engage in political activities that could potentially compromise their objectivity and impartiality in their roles within the organization. Such a provision aims to prevent conflicts between an employee's political affiliations or activities and their professional duties, ensuring that employees act in the best interests of the organization and avoid situations where their political beliefs or associations could interfere with their work.
  • Any employee who is in this situation must disclose the conflict to the CEO in writing detailing the full facts.  Failure to do so will subject the concerned employee to  disciplinary action by the Management.

Prohibited actions

  • Personal or sexual harassment is unethical, illegal and unacceptable at COMPANY.  Any employee found guilty of this misconduct shall be subject to severe disciplinary measure.
  • Use, peddling or distribution of controlled / prohibited drugs within the Company premises.
  • Any employee who is proven to have used or is using prohibited drugs shall be subjected to disciplinary measures (including termination).  Moreover, the erring employee shall be turned over to the local authorities for appropriate action.
  • Alcohol or any toxicating drink/public drunkenness within any of the Company premises is strictly prohibited. Any person who engages in this act shall be subject to strict disciplinary measures.

Disciplinary Actions

Suspension

  • This refers to a disciplinary action resulting in the temporary cessation in the performance of an employee’s duties and responsibilities
  • Suspensions are without pay.  While on suspension, employees may be barred from entering COMPANY’s premises.  At COMPANY, light suspension shall be five (5) working days while severe suspension shall be upward of fifteen (15) working days.
  • Preventive suspension may be administered by COMPANY if there is reason to believe that the continued presence of the employee within the premises of the company will pose a serious and imminent threat to his co-employees, management and / or to company property.
  • The pay during suspension shall be in accordance with Labour Laws.

Demotion

  • This refers to the downward change or movement of an employee. It means a decrease in rank, authority and/or responsibility.
  • Grounds for demotion include inefficiency, incompetence, and/or commission of offenses punishable under the Code of Conduct and Behavior but which does not result in the termination of services.

Termination

  • This refers to the cessation of employer-employee relationship initiated by COMPANY for a just or authorized cause in accordance with law.  All termination cases based on just cause are subject to review and approval by the CEO.
  • An employee whose employment is terminated for cause shall forfeit all the benefits and privileges due to him (except those required by law) and may be subjected to legal action by COMPANY as warranted or deemed necessary.

Removal and / or other disciplinary action

The Company shall have the right to dismiss or discharge an employee without any compensation whatsoever, should he be considered guilty of breach of his service agreement, insobriety, addiction to drugs, dishonesty, and neglect of duty or conduct considered detrimental to the interest of the company.

The Company may also take any other disciplinary action at its sole discretion for misconduct. The employees of the Company are expected to conduct themselves in accordance with the rules and regulations governing the service terms and conditions as applicable from time to time.

Loan & Advance

Loan & Advance Policy:

Eligibility:

  1. Permanent employees are eligible for loans after completing 1 year of service, while advances can be availed after 6 months.
  2. An employee can avail a loan up to 3 times during their tenure with the company.
  3. An advance can be taken a maximum of 3 times in a calendar year.
  4. There must be a minimum of 6 months between loan avails.
  5. Loan and advance requests should be made between the 10th and 20th of the respective month.

Repayment:

  1. The loan repayment period is a minimum of 6 months and a maximum of 12 months.
  2. Advance repayment will be deducted from the salary for the particular month in which it is availed.

The Act defines “Retrenchment” as the termination by the employer of the services of an employee for any reason whatsoever, otherwise than as a punishment inflicted by way of disciplinary action, but doesn't include-

(a) Voluntary retirement of the employee; or

(b) Retirement of the employee on reaching the age of superannuation if the contract of employment between the employer and the employee concerned contains a stipulation in that behalf; or

Termination of the service of the employee  as a result of the on-renewal of the contract of employment between the employer and the employee concerned on its expiry or of such contract being terminated under a stipulation in that behalf contained therein; or

(c) Termination of the service of a employee on the ground of continued ill-health;

Here, the key ingredient is the termination of an employee from service, by the employer. This does not mean the employer can retrench an employee as a punishment by way of disciplinary action. Further, this scenario strictly does not include the above-mentioned conditions contemplated under the subsection.

  1. Sexual Harassment Policy

Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish a safe and respectful work environment that is free from any form of sexual harassment. This policy aims to define the scope of sexual harassment, outline the complaint procedures, establish a redressal process, and highlight the responsibilities of the Internal Complaints Committee (ICC).

Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, vendors, clients, visitors, or any other individuals associated with VIPL, whether in the workplace, at off-site locations, or during any work-related activities or events. It covers all forms of sexual harassment that may occur within the organization or in connection with its activities.

Complaints procedure

  • Reporting: Any employee who experiences or witnesses sexual harassment should report the incident promptly to their immediate reporting manager, the Human Resources department, or any designated person responsible for handling such complaints.
  • Confidentiality: All complaints will be treated with strict confidentiality, and the identity of the complainant, witnesses, and the accused will be protected to the extent possible.
  • Investigation: The company will conduct a fair and impartial investigation into all complaints of sexual harassment. The investigation will be conducted promptly, ensuring a safe environment for all parties involved.
  • Disciplinary Action: If sexual harassment is substantiated after the investigation, appropriate disciplinary action will be taken against the offender in accordance with the company's policies and applicable laws.
  • No Retaliation: The company strictly prohibits any form of retaliation against individuals who report sexual harassment or participate in the investigation process.

Redressal process

  • Receipt of Complaint:
  • Any employee who experiences or witnesses sexual harassment should promptly report the incident to their reporting manager, Human Resources (HR) department.
  • Complaints can be made orally or in writing, and the complainant should provide relevant details, including date, time, location, and a description of the incident(s) to the designated person.
  • Confidentiality and Support:
  • The designated person will ensure strict confidentiality throughout the process, protecting the privacy of the complainant, witnesses, and the accused to the extent possible.
  • The complainant will be provided with information about support services available, such as counselling, employee assistance programs, or legal advice, if required.
  • Investigation
  • The Investigation Committee will conduct a fair and unbiased investigation, ensuring the rights of all parties involved.
  • The complainant, witnesses, and the accused will be interviewed separately, and relevant evidence will be collected.
  • The investigation process will be completed within a reasonable timeframe while ensuring the thoroughness of the investigation.
  • The Investigation Committee will analyze the evidence gathered and determine if sexual harassment has occurred based on the preponderance of evidence standard.
  • If sexual harassment is established, the Investigation Committee will recommend appropriate disciplinary actions in line with the company's policies and applicable laws.
  • The recommendations may include warnings, suspension, termination, or any other appropriate measures to address the situation and prevent future incidents.

  • Communication and Follow-Up
  • The HR department or designated person will communicate the outcome of the investigation and any actions taken to the complainant, the accused, and relevant stakeholders in a confidential manner.
  • The HR department will provide necessary support and assistance to the complainant, ensuring their well-being and facilitating their reintegration into the workplace.
  • Regular Review and Training
  • The company will periodically review the effectiveness of the sexual harassment policy and the redressal process to ensure continuous improvement.
  • Regular training programs will be conducted to educate employees about their rights, responsibilities, and the prevention of sexual harassment in the workplace.

Internal Complaints Committee

The company will establish an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) consisting of at least four members, two employee representatives (preferably including one woman).

  • The ICC will be responsible for receiving and addressing complaints of sexual harassment, conducting investigations, and recommending appropriate actions based on the findings.
  • ICC members will receive regular training on the prevention of sexual harassment, complaint handling procedures, and the legal framework surrounding sexual harassment in the workplace
